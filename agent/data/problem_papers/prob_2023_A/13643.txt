1.1 Background
The dandelion is a very common plant with scientific name Taraxacum officinale, which is
stemless, lactiferous and perennial[17]. We can find them at roadsides, in wild fields or at incon-

evolving on this planet for about 30 million years[5] - even longer than we human bMeings!
But currently they are labeled as an invasive plant. Invasive species is a relative concept. It
means a non-native species whose introduction does or is likely to cause economic or environmental
C

them invasive. They can adapt to different environments. They have high growth rate and spread

Also, they have deep taproots that enable them to pull available water out of the soil. It is also

worldwide[17]. H

3.1 Assumptions and Justifications
号号
1. A plant’s impact has a positive correlation with its biological characteristics.
Justification: Study shows that the invasiveness of a plant species is closely related to its
众
performance-related traits[22]. Thus we can think of biological characteristics as fairly
众
reliable indicators on the invasiveness, which is the plant’s ability to cause harm to the
environment and公to humans.
2. A plant’s impac公t can be lessened by its benefits.
Justification:信Ifplantsareedibleforanimalsorbeneficialfortheenvironmentinsomeother
ways, it is likely that the environment will be subject to less harm overall. Likewise,
if p信lants can be made into products or utilized in other ways, they can benefit the
微
economy and cause less harm to humans.
3. At locations with higher population density, the impact of a plant is greater.
微
Justification: At places with higher population density, it is likely that people try harder to
control undesirable species. This leads to more expenditure, which means the impact
is greater. At places with low population density, there is less human intervention.
Non-native plants may reach an equilibrium with native ones, thus causing less impact.

Our Vigorous Friend
It is always a dreamlike scene to see dandelion seeds flying on a summer day. All those little
parachutes drift in the air, slowly and gently, through a beam of light, like angels from the sky.
We know how children love dandelions. They never tire of blowing puffballs and running after
the parachutes. But we also know how adults dislike them. The dandelions cause allergy, grow
with our crops, and spread like mad. Most of the time, we label them as invasive hMerbs that need
great effort to take care of.
Whether we like them or not, dandelions spread all over the world. They can adapt to almost
C

all kinds of environments, regardless of what the soil quality, humidity and temperature are. They
grow fast, have abundant seeds and propagate without relying on other creatures. They never tire
M
of dispersing, germinating and flowering. Looking at them, we have to Cadmit they are so vigorous
and full of vitality.

even invasive species can reach an equilibrium wi：th others. At some points, the invasive species
could develop very fast and occupy a lot of resources, but gradually, when other species go into
competition sufficiently, all the species will get along in harmony and share our planet peacefully.
