Transmission rate: This refers to how easily a disease is able to spread from someone
who is already infected to someone who is susceptible to the disease. Mathematically, it
is defined as the probability that any interaction between a healthy, non-immune person
and a victim of the disease will result in the healthy person contracting the disease. As
such, this value ranges from 0 to 1, with a lower value meaning that a disease only is
transmitted on rare occasions, and a higher value meaning that the disease is passed on at
nearly every interaction between an infected and susceptible person.
M

during which they are infectious, that is they can transmit the disease to others, but are
C
not displaying symptoms. While someone is in this latent stage, they are extremely
M
dangerous because they might continue to engage in regular interactions with others
while both they and those that they come in contact with arei oblivious to the fact that
H
they are exposing others or are being exposed to a deadly disease. For our purposes, the

众

公
