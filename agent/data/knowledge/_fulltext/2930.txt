Q: What is the ISS? What is its purpose?
A: The ISS is a satellite in low Earth orbit, which normally houses six astronauts

in a zero gravity environment. It was established to help scientists

hardware malfunction, spacewalks are conducted. But either way, these
costs are negligible in comparison to the overall scheme of things.

In order to develop a comprehensive flight schedule, we needed to analyze the
amount of payload needed per year and optimal conditions for launching. The
most important factor dealing with optimal launching conditions is that the ISS

coordinates of 28˚ 29’ N and 80˚ 34’ W. We selected this launch facility because it
is the ISS’s main launching facility.
